Data as a lens on human experience

The Atlas of Human Experience is a collection of data visualizations exploring what it means to be human — how we sleep and dream, where we move, what we fear, and what brings us joy.

Each piece starts with a question rather than a dataset. The data is gathered second, chosen to illuminate rather than to impress.

The dream globe

The dream visualization draws on cross-cultural psychology research, including the Sleep and Dream Database (SDDb) and published academic studies on recurring dream themes. The regional groupings and counts are illustrative — grounded in real research patterns but not a direct download of any single dataset.

The globe is built with Three.js for 3D rendering, TopoJSON for real country geometry, and vanilla JavaScript for everything else. No frameworks.

Data sources

Sleep and Dream Database sleepanddreamdatabase.org — open access, 44,500+ dream reports
DreamBank dreambank.net — Domhoff & Schneider, open dream corpus
Natural Earth / TopoJSON world-atlas — country geometry, open license
